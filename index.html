間 What wants to be born
but can't find its way.

There is something in you that wants to emerge. That has been waiting. Not because you haven't tried, but because the answer keeps coming from the same place: the mind, the will, the urge to do more, add more, find the right method, the right practice, the right form.

We live submerged. In information, injunctions, tools, philosophies, ideologies, art forms. Layer after layer of porridge. And what is meant to be born suffocates inside it.

The only thing that can allow the essence of what wants to emerge to actually take form is emptiness. The space between things. Ma.

The desert itself is a vast field of 間 Ma. Not silence, not emptiness, not timelessness. Within it exists the frequency beneath silence, the emergence of life from apparent nothingness, a presence not yet fully appeared, yet already here.

Desert Mirage Journey is the space you offer yourself. To let the mirage come alive. To give yourself room, real room, to be fully traversed by life. To free yourself, for a time, from the noise, the overflow, the constant pull of what you should be doing or becoming.

In this journey, the only thing that will truly matter is listening. Listening to what asks to be expressed. To what asks to be born. Without judgment.

This is not a workshop. Not a stage. Not a course with a curriculum. It is a space of emergence, of artistic co-creation. And to create that space, we hold the posture of the midwife. A midwife does not decide what is born. She does not know in advance what will come. She simply stays present, trusts the process, and holds the space for it to happen. That is our role here, so that what is unknown in you can emerge. And grow.

The Process

Not a programme.
A journey into the unknown.

Three movements. No script. What happens in between is yours.

01
Arriving

Marrakech. The city, the medina, the noise. A first breath of this culture before the silence ahead. The body starts to land. You begin to arrive.

02
Walking into emptiness

Eight days in the Sahara. Nomadic walking, bivouac under stars, dunes, silence. The desert strips what is unnecessary. Space opens. What was suffocating begins to breathe.

03
What emerges

Embodied practices, co-creation, artistic exploration. Not a performance to produce. An encounter with what has been waiting. The midwives hold the space. The rest is yours.

2026 — Your Facilitators

Butoh dancer & choreographer
Yi Chen

Born in China, Yi Chen is a Butoh-based dancer, choreographer, and teacher whose work explores the space bridging the visible and invisible. Body is the movement; space is the dance.

Co-creator of the Desert Mirage Project since its first edition. She has walked this desert before — and each time, differently.

"Butoh builds a bridge, in between the visible and invisible, an expanded life concept."

Practitioner & initiatory guide
Anys

Painter, dancer, and organiser of desert retreats and initiatory experiences. His work — on paper and through the body — draws from Butoh, Tao, calligraphy, and elemental movement. Each performance is conceived as a ritual: a psychomagical act that bridges the personal and the universal.

Franco-Moroccan, nomadic by nature. The Sahara is not a destination for him — it is a place of return.

Eco-lodge built with sandbag, straw clay, and adobe by nomad hands. Warm at night, cool by day — it breathes.

Sahara — Nights 4–7
Desert Bivouac

Sleep under a shared nomad tent or under the Milky Way. Universe as roof, spacious desert as ground, silence as lullaby.
