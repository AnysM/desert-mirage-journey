間 Ma is not emptiness.
It is the tension between events — already present, not yet manifested.

In the Japanese context, 「間（Ma）」 is not a concept that can be definitively defined, but a relational state that is practiced, judged, and perceived within specific situations. Ma is a state that is felt, breathed, and waited for.

Ma is the moment when light passes through the gap of a door. Space that is temporal, and time that is spatial. The tension between events — a generative state within relationships, a potential space that can be reactivated.

Butoh does not produce form. It allows the body to enter a state of Being Moved. When intention gives way, when the body is drawn by what is invisible, form emerges naturally within 間.

December 2026 — Artistic Residency

間 is the teacher.
We are here to listen.

This edition is not structured around teaching or technique. It is an open-ended artistic residency — a shared entry into 間. The desert sets the rhythm. No fixed curriculum. No performance to produce. Only presence, movement, and what emerges in the space between.

To become an empty body does not mean wishing to think of nothing, imagining oneself as such, or attempting to become a body that abandons everything. What You Will Live

Other Experience

Dance is not only on the stage — wellness comes from body, mind and spirit.

01
Jamaa el Fna

Experience this UNESCO Masterpiece of Oral Heritage. Storytellers, musicians, performers — the square is a living spectacle that changes daily.

02
Exotic Landscape

From the High Atlas Mountains to the Date Palm Valley, culminating in the vast Sahara. Visit Aït Benhaddou and Ouarzazate along the way.

03
Meeting the Tuareg

The nomads of the sands. Ali, founder of Desert Flower, will guide you — a man born in the desert, a camel shepherd and desert shaman.

04
Solitude in the Sahara

Walk alone in the vast desert, listening to the language of the universe. Deepen body training in absolute silence, without distractions.

05
Desert Flower Base

An international platform for sustainable development in southern Morocco, operated by the local Tuareg — your gateway to the desert journey.

06
Noguchi Taiso

Explore the natural principles of movement — spiral, wave, gravity. The main actors are not the muscles but body weight and its counter-reaction.

Programme

December 10 – 21
12 Days · Morocco

J1 — Dec 10
Marrakech

Arrival · Riad accommodation in the medina. Settle in, first contact with the city before the silence ahead.

J2 — Dec 11
Route to the Desert

Long drive south from Marrakech. The landscape empties. Mountains, valley, then sand. Arrival at camp.

J3 — Dec 12
Walk + Bivouac

First day of nomadic walking. Body meets sand. First night under open sky.

J4 — Dec 13
Walk + Bivouac

Deeper into the dunes. Silence grows. The Ma between steps becomes audible.

J5 — Dec 14
Fixed Bivouac in the Dunes

No movement today. Staying. The body learns to inhabit stillness — the fullness of 間.

J6 — Dec 15
Fixed Bivouac in the Dunes

Second day of dwelling in place. Butoh practice in the open desert. Stars, sand, body.

J7 — Dec 16
Walk + Bivouac

Moving again. The body carries the two days of stillness. Walking differently now.

J8 — Dec 17
Walk + Arrival at Fixed Camp

Last nomadic day. Return to a fixed camp. A roof appears. The body registers the transition.

J9 — Dec 18
Integration + Fixed Camp

Circle of sharing. Questions. Rest. The desert experience begins to settle within.

J10 — Dec 19
Ruined Village + Performances

Visit to a village in ruins. Site-specific Butoh performances. Documented by professional photographer.

J11 — Dec 20
Route back to Marrakech

Long drive north. The city returns. Riad accommodation — one last night in the medina.

J12 — Dec 21
Marrakech to Airport

Transfer to Marrakech airport. You carry the desert with you. 間 remains.

2026 — Your Facilitators

Yi Chen & Anys Neoqii
will accompany you.

Butoh dancer & choreographer
Yi Chen

Born in China, Yi Chen is a Butoh-based dancer, choreographer, and teacher whose work explores the space bridging the visible and invisible. Body is the movement; space is the dance.

Co-creator of the Desert Mirage Project since its first edition. She has walked this desert before — and each time, differently.

"Butoh builds a bridge, in between the visible and invisible, an expanded life concept."

Practitioner & initiatory guide
Anys Neoqii

Franco-Moroccan practitioner working at the intersection of human accompaniment, somatic exploration, and creative expression. Co-organizer of men's initiatory retreats in Morocco, Ardèche, and the Moroccan Sahara.

The desert is his ancestral land — a space where the body unlearns what civilization has imposed.
